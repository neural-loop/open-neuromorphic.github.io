Neuromorphic computing in medicine applies brain-inspired principles to solve complex healthcare challenges. This field ranges from developing ultra-low-power processors for wearable health monitors and smart prosthetics to creating large-scale neural simulations for studying diseases like epilepsy or Alzheimer's. By leveraging event-driven processing and on-chip learning, neuromorphic technologies are paving the way for more efficient, real-time, and personalized medical solutions.
